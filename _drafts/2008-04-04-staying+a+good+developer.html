--- layout: post title: Staying a good developer date: '2008-04-04T12:08:00.006Z' author: Tim Abell tags: - dev - opinion modified_time: '2008-04-10T17:28:24.875Z' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-5082828566240519947.post-3510278914296316659 --- A friend asked me how I stay a good developer. Apart from being deeply flattered, I was rather lost for an answer, so thinking that this is a very worthy question I spent some time putting some thoughts together on what might contribute to this.

It's a different, and much harder to answer question than how you become a good developer in the first place. The question implies that the knowledge and attitude that makes someone a good developer is already present, which rules out a lot of obvious answers such as learning Java or C#, and knowing how to use source control such as subversion or git.

So here goes:
  • Spend as much time with your peers as possible.
  • Try and understand the business's real needs and understand how programming has value outside of the IT world.
  • Contribute to an open source project. This will expose you to unrestrained peer review, and allow you to review your assumptions.
  • Explain your ideas and understandings to others, of all levels of technical knowledge. Trying to explain concepts will show up holes in your understanding, and assumptions that you can then work on removing.
  • Try to keep up with the ever growing list of available tools. Sometimes a framework can save work and open up new possibilities. Missing out on these makes you less competitive, even though they are never a panacea.
Seek out facts that contradict your mental model of the world.
